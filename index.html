How Bitcoin Works

Bitcoin is a decentralized network that allows peer-to-peer value transfer without banks.

Bitcoin uses SHA-256 Cryptography. Every 10 minutes, miners compete to solve a puzzle. The winner adds a block of transactions to the ledger and is rewarded with new BTC. This process is called "Proof of Work," ensuring the network cannot be forged or manipulated.

Digital vs Traditional Money

Why "Hard Money" is replacing government-issued fiat.

Fiat money (USD/EUR) has an infinite supply, leading to inflation. Bitcoin has a fixed supply of 21 million. Furthermore, traditional money is "permission-based"—banks can freeze your account. Crypto is "permissionless"—if you hold the keys, you have total control.

Crypto Folklore & Legends

THE PIZZA DAY

The $700,000,000 Papa John's Order

In 2010, Laszlo Hanyecz bought two pizzas for 10,000 BTC. It was the first time Bitcoin was used to buy a physical product. Today, those pizzas would be worth nearly a billion dollars.

THE WOW NERF

How World of Warcraft Created Ethereum

Vitalik Buterin created Ethereum after game developers nerfed his favorite character. He realized centralized power was dangerous and built a decentralized world computer.

SATOSHI

The Ghost in the Machine

The creator of Bitcoin, Satoshi Nakamoto, vanished in 2011. He left behind 1.1 million BTC (worth $70B+) that has never been touched, ensuring Bitcoin remains a leaderless movement.
