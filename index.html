What Is Crypto?

Cryptocurrency is a form of decentralized money that only exists in digital form. Unlike dollars, it isn’t issued or controlled by a government or central bank. Instead, it runs on a blockchain: a public ledger spread across a network of computers.

The Digital Spreadsheet

“Blockchain is similar to a spreadsheet everyone can see but no one can change,” says Ron Levy. It ensures ownership and supply are verifiable without a bank.

Mining & Validation

Computers solve complex puzzles to validate transactions. The first to solve it earns new coins, supporting the network's security.

Investing Made Easy

Key Takeaways

  • Market Adoption: U.S. ownership grew from 2% (2018) to 17% (2025).
  • 24/7 Trading: Unlike stocks, crypto markets never close.
  • Tax Status: The IRS treats crypto as property; selling is a taxable event.
  • Volatility: Rapid price swings are common due to 24/7 supply/demand.
17%

Of U.S. investors now own cryptocurrency as of 2025.

Why People Invest

Confidence in blockchain technology, speculation on price increases, or as a hedge against inflation in traditional markets.

Technology & Trust

“Blockchain changes how we think about trust in finance,” says Charley Brady. It enables genuine peer-to-peer value transfer globally without traditional banks.

Decentralization

No single point of failure. The network is maintained by thousands of independent participants.

Smart Contracts

Self-executing agreements that trigger automatically when conditions are met, removing the need for lawyers or intermediaries.

Crypto Legends & Stories

The history of cryptocurrency is filled with fascinating, and sometimes bizarre, turning points.

The Bitcoin Pizza

The $700 Million Dinner

In 2010, Laszlo Hanyecz paid 10,000 BTC for two Papa John's pizzas. At the time, it was worth $41. At Bitcoin's peak, those pizzas would be worth over $700,000,000. This was the first real-world transaction using cryptocurrency.

The Ethereum Vision

Vitalik Buterin's Motivation

The creator of Ethereum, Vitalik Buterin, reportedly wanted to create a decentralized system after a centralized gaming company (Blizzard) nerfed his favorite character's "Siphon Life" spell in World of Warcraft. He realized how much power centralized entities have and decided to build a world where users hold the power.

Satoshi Nakamoto

The Ghost Creator

The creator of Bitcoin remains anonymous to this day. Satoshi disappeared in 2011, leaving behind a fortune of 1.1 million BTC that has never been moved. This anonymity ensures Bitcoin has no "head" to be attacked by governments.

Storage & Safety

You need a wallet and an exchange account to start, but you can begin with a very small amount of money.

Cold Storage

Physical devices (USB-like) that keep your keys offline. The gold standard for security.

Exchange Wallets

Easiest to use, but the exchange controls your funds. Ideal for small amounts or active trading.

Security Rules

  • Never share your 12-24 word recovery phrase.
  • Always enable 2FA (Two-Factor Authentication).
  • Verify every address before hitting "Send".

Essential Vocabulary

HODL

A misspelling of "hold" that became a mantra for long-term investors who refuse to sell during price drops.

Fiat

Traditional government-issued currency like the US Dollar or Euro.

Gas Fees

The transaction fee paid to miners or validators to process your transaction on the network.

Stablecoin

A digital asset pegged 1:1 to a stable asset like the Dollar (e.g., USDC, USDT).
